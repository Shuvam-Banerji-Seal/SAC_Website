SAC Placement Cell

The SAC Placement Cell is the student-run body under SAC Academics that coordinates the campus placement season at IISER Kolkata. Acting as the bridge between the institute's career office, the visiting companies, and the graduating cohort, the cell takes responsibility for every stage of the recruitment drive — from announcing recruiters and circulating resumes to running the Pre-Placement Talks, handling logistics on the day of each visit, and publishing the final selection results.

The committee is composed of an Overall Coordinator, Department Coordinators drawn from each academic stream (Physical Sciences, Chemical Sciences, Biological Sciences, Mathematics and Statistics, and Earth Sciences), and a small group of Department Volunteers who help with scheduling, communications, and on-the-day coordination. The current term runs for the 2025-26 academic year.

How the Placement Process Works

The campus placement process at IISER Kolkata is run jointly by the Career Development Cell and the SAC Placement Student Committee. The committee handles the student-facing parts of the season — communications, scheduling, and on-the-day logistics — so that every eligible candidate has a clear, consistent experience from the announcement of a visiting company to the final offer.

The typical recruitment cycle

  1. Announcement. The committee circulates the recruiter's job description and eligibility criteria across the relevant batches. A short registration window follows, after which the resumes are forwarded to the company.
  2. Pre-Placement Talk. Most companies host a PPT in the lecture-hall complex before the assessment. Attendance is optional but strongly recommended — it is the easiest way to read the team, the role, and the expectations for the day.
  3. Online Assessment. Written or online tests are usually scheduled in the central computer lab or the institute auditorium, with timings shared a day in advance.
  4. Interviews. Shortlisted candidates are called for technical and HR rounds. The committee coordinates interview slots, room allocations, and panel logistics on the day of the visit.
  5. Results. Final selections are announced through the committee's official channels. The Career Development Cell then processes the offers and onboarding paperwork.

Who can participate

Participation is open to all registered BS-MS and PhD students of IISER Kolkata who are in their final year and meet the eligibility criteria shared by the recruiter. Students in earlier years may register for internships where the company permits, subject to academic-load considerations.

What the committee handles

  • Communicating job descriptions, deadlines, and shortlists to students.
  • Coordinating Pre-Placement Talks, assessments, and interview schedules.
  • Reserving rooms, equipment, and technical support for each visit.
  • Acting as a first point of contact for student concerns during the recruitment season.
  • Maintaining a feedback channel with the Career Development Cell so that process issues raised in one season are addressed in the next.
