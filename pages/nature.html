Nature Club — IISER Kolkata

The Nature Club of IISER Kolkata fosters creative expression through photography and visual storytelling, while nurturing a deep appreciation for the natural world among students. The club brings together individuals who share a passion for nature, wildlife, and environmental conservation.

The club’s core aims are to encourage interest in nature and wildlife, organize campus cleanliness drives, document the rich biodiversity of the IISER Kolkata campus, and educate the student community about climate change and sustainable living.

Through regular nature walks, photography sessions, and awareness campaigns, the Nature Club strives to connect students with the environment around them and inspire meaningful action towards its preservation.

Aims & Objectives

  • Encourage interest in nature and wildlife among the student community
  • Organize campus cleanliness drives and environmental awareness campaigns
  • Document the biodiversity of the IISER Kolkata campus through photography
  • Educate students about climate change, sustainability, and conservation

Events

The Nature Club organizes a variety of events throughout the year that bring students closer to the outdoors and raise environmental awareness on campus.

Eco-Trail (Trail-Quest)

Eco-Trail is a signature event of the Nature Club that combines adventure with ecology. Participants embark on guided trails through the campus and surrounding areas, learning about local flora, fauna, and ecosystems along the way. The trail-quest format adds a competitive element with nature-themed challenges and identification tasks.

Field Trips

The club organizes regular field trips to nearby nature reserves, wetlands, and biodiversity hotspots. These trips provide students with hands-on experiences of wildlife observation, bird watching, and nature photography, helping them develop a deeper understanding of the natural world.

Achievements

  • Conservation of campus biodiversity through documentation and awareness
  • Engaging and informative stalls at open days and campus events
  • Regular nature documentation projects capturing the ecological diversity of the campus
  • Active participation in environmental initiatives and sustainability campaigns

Rules & Regulations

Equipment Lending

Club equipment (cameras, binoculars, etc.) may be borrowed by members for academic or club-related purposes. Items must be returned within a maximum of 12 days. A fine of Rs 50 per day will be levied for late returns. All equipment must be handled with care and returned in the same condition as received.

Camera Handling

Cameras and other sensitive equipment should be handled with utmost care. Members must ensure that equipment is not exposed to water, dust, or rough handling. Any damage to club property must be reported immediately and may be subject to repair or replacement costs.

Event Guidelines

All members are expected to participate actively in club events and activities. Regular attendance at meetings and events is encouraged. Members should maintain discipline and cooperate with the organizing team during all club activities.

Club Assets

  • 1 Camera
  • 4 Binoculars
